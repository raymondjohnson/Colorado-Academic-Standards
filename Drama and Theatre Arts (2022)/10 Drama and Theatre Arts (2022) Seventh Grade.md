# Seventh Grade | Drama and Theatre Arts

## Standard 1. Create

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.1.1: Generate and conceptualize artistic ideas and work.

#### Prepared Graduates

1. Theatre artists rely on intuition, curiosity, and critical inquiry.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Integrate multiple perspectives and solutions to staging challenges in a drama/theater work.
2. Explain and present possible solutions to design challenges in a drama/theater work.
3. Envision and describe a scripted or improvised character's inner thoughts and objectives in a drama/theater work.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Interpersonal Communication
2. Creativity and Innovation
3. Self-Advocacy and Initiative

##### Inquiry Questions:

1. Why does the location of the audience matter when telling a story in theatre? (Aligns with EO a.)
2. How do you come to a solution (Aligns with EO a.)
3. What challenges does the script present and how might you overcome them? (Aligns with EO b.)
4. What are your character's inner thoughts? How do you know? (Aligns with EO c.)

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.1.2: Organize and develop artistic ideas and work.

#### Prepared Graduates

2. Theatre artists work to discover different ways of communicating meaning.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Interpersonal Communication

3. Social Awareness

##### Inquiry Questions:

2. How do ensembles accomplish their goal? (Aligns with EO b.)

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.1.3: Connect artistic ideas to personal experience and varied perspectives.

#### Prepared Graduates

3. Theatre artists seek awareness of interrelationships between self and others to influence and inform their work.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Consider incorporating multiple perspectives and diverse community ideas in a drama/theatre work.
2. Examine research relevant to the drama/theatre work to better understand performance and design choices.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Critical Thinking and Analysis
2. Social Awareness
3. Self-Awareness

##### Inquiry Questions:

1. How can you show respect for a space and the people who have worked to put together a performance? (Aligns with EO a.)
2. How can research be used to enhance a drama/theatre work? (Aligns with EO b.)

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.1.4: Refine towards completion of artistic work.

#### Prepared Graduates

4. Theatre artists refine their work and practice their craft through rehearsal.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Demonstrate focus and concentration in the rehearsal process to analyze and refine choices in a devised or scripted drama/theatre work.
2. Develop effective physical and vocal traits of characters in an improvised or scripted drama/theatre work.
3. Implement planned technical design elements during the rehearsal process for a devised or scripted drama/theatre work.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Interpersonal Communication
2. Collaboration and Teamwork
3. Perseverance and Resilience

##### Inquiry Questions:

1. How does staying in character enhance the rehearsal process? (Aligns with EO a.)
2. Why should you experiment with different choices for your character? (Aligns with EO b.)
3. What effect does one technical element have on another?" (Aligns with EO c.)

## Standard 3. Critically Respond

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.3.1: Perceive and analyze artistic work.

#### Prepared Graduates

9. Theatre artists reflect to understand the impact of drama processes and theatre experiences.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Compare recorded personal and peer reactions to artistic choices in a drama/ theatre work.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Media Literacy
2. Critical Thinking and Analysis
3. Self-Awareness

##### Inquiry Questions:

1. Why is criticism better than judgment?  (Aligns with EO a.)
2. Do you hear your peers making fair, grounded criticisms and providing clear meaningful feedback? (Aligns with EO a.)
3. What is the difference between meaningful critique and not? (Aligns with EO a.)
4. How can you bypass your initial (first) reaction to have a meaningful critique? (Aligns with EO a.)

### Grade Level Expectation DT.7.3.2: Interpret intent and meaning in artistic work.

#### Prepared Graduates

10. Theatre artists' interpretations of drama/theatre work are influenced by personal experiences and aesthetics.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Identify the artistic choices made based on personal experience in a drama/theatre work.
2. Describe how cultural perspectives can influence the evaluation of drama/theatre work.
3. Interpret how the use of personal aesthetics, preferences, and beliefs can be used to discuss drama/theatre work.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Data Literacy
2. Critical Thinking and Analysis
3. Global and Cultural Awareness
4. Empowered Individual: Self-Awareness

##### Inquiry Questions:

1. Why do you think the artists made their choice? (Aligns with EO a.)
2. How does someone else's perspective differ from yours? (Aligns with EO b.)
3. What other experiences in your life can you relate to the piece of theatre you just saw? (Aligns with EO c.)
