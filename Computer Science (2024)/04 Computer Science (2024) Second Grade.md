# Second Grade | Computer Science

## Standard 2.  Computing Systems and Networks

### Grade Level Expectation CS.2.2.1:  When developing a plan or solution, the designer must follow specifications designated for the program.

#### Prepared Graduates

5.  Develop systems solutions from a set of specifications to complete a design process.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Develop plans that describe a program’s sequence of events, goals, and expected outcomes.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Articulate task requirements for a solution based on stakeholder input. (Professional Skills, Task/Time Management)

##### Computer Science Practices:

1. Communicating about Computing
2. Creating Computational Artifacts

## Standard 3.  Computer Programming

### Grade Level Expectation CS.2.3.1:  A computer program is a sequence of steps expressed in a computer programming language so that a computer can follow and perform the steps.

#### Prepared Graduates

7.  Design and create programs, individually and collaboratively, for a variety of disciplines.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Select and use appropriate software to perform a variety of tasks and recognize that users have different needs and preferences for the technology they use.
2. Develop programs with sequences and simple loops, to express ideas or address a problem.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Civic/Interpersonal Skills, Collaboration/Teamwork

##### Computer Science Practices:

1. Creating Computational Artifacts

## Standard 5.  Artificial Intelligence (AI)

### Grade Level Expectation CS.2.5.1:  AI tools can perform intelligent tasks, such as recognition of patterns, decision-making, and classification of information, that help users understand the world.

#### Prepared Graduates

10.  Use AI tools to analyze and understand the world and to create and inspire ideas.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Demonstrate how to train a computer to recognize something.
2. Describe and provide examples of how people learn and how computers learn.
3. Describe the kinds of tasks an intelligent assistant can and cannot perform.
4. Describe some jobs that no longer exist due to advances in technology.
5. Identify devices in daily life that use AI technologies.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Ask questions and learn about careers, including how those careers might be impacted by advances in technology (Professional Skills, Career Awareness).

##### Computer Science Practices:

1. Recognizing and Defining Computational Problems
2. Developing and Using Abstractions
3. Collaborating about Computing

### Grade Level Expectation CS.2.5.2:  AI tools can be designed for a variety of purposes and can impact people differently based on how they are designed.

#### Prepared Graduates

11.  Evaluate the uses of AI.

#### Evidence Outcomes

##### Students Can:

1. Examine a labeled dataset and identify problems in the data that could lead a computer to make incorrect predictions.
2. Identify current uses of AI and how they have impacted people.
3. Describe how AI can be used to solve problems that affect people.

#### Academic Contexts and Connections

##### Colorado Essential Skills:

1. Identify and reflect upon personal connections to AI and how it impacts society. (Global/Cultural Awareness, Civic Engagement)

##### Computer Science Practices:

1. Recognizing and Defining Computational Problems
2. Communicating about Computing
3. Fostering an Inclusive Computing Culture

## Standard 6.  Digital Citizenship

##### Computer Science Practices:

1. Fostering an Inclusive Computing Culture
